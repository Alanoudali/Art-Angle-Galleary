    The Sick Child
    1907

    The Sick Child draws upon Munch’s memory of his sister Sophie’s death from tuberculosis at the age of fifteen.
    The model was a young girl who Munch had observed sitting distraught when he accompanied his father, a doctor, to treat her brother’s broken leg.
    Munch worked on the painting for a year, developing the rapid brushwork and vivid colour that suggest the painful evocation of a traumatic memory.
